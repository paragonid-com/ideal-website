resultsTitle: "WHAT TO EXPECT\nDURING THE TREATMENT"
resultsBody: |
  Each regenerative medicine procedure differs from one to the next. With PRP, a blood sample is drawn and then put into a centrifuge, separating the blood components from the platelets to create a unique matrix. Then, the solution is injected into the scalp (or other target areas) to target hair loss. As the growth factors in the plasma are released, they will help jumpstart hair growth and help with overall healing and scalp health. PRP results are usually noticed about six months after a full course of treatment and will require regular maintenance to maintain the results.

  With an allograft injection, the donor tissues or cells, usually obtained from cord blood, amniotic fluid, or Wharton's jelly, are first prepared and processed. Then, using local anesthesia, the area where the allograft will be injected is numbed. Next, the allograft is injected into the specific treatment area where healing and repair are required. Since allograft injections contain growth factors, proteins, and other natural elements, they work to help heal the area and promote tissue regeneration.

  As the treatment area recovers and heals, the condition that was treated begins to improve. Some people experience mild swelling and discomfort for the first few days as the area starts to repair itself. Then, over time, the allograft allows for new cell growth and decreased inflammation while supporting tissue regeneration.
