## How Tirzepatide Works

Tirzepatide is a weekly injectable weight loss medication used for weight loss and weight management. While initially used to help those with diabetes manage their condition, it has the added benefit of aiding in weight loss when used for up to 16 weeks.

Tirzepatide is a GLP-1 receptor agonist (glucagon-like peptide-1). It helps you to lose weight by mimicking the actions of the hormone GLP-1, which regulates hunger in the body. Tirzepatide tells the brain that the stomach is full, reducing how much food you eat and leading to weight loss. When patients ask, “Does tirzepatide suppress the appetite?” It does. The medication is also known to slow down the rate at which food is emptied into the stomach, leaving you feeling fuller for longer and with fewer food cravings.

Since each person’s weight loss journey is different, as is their starting weight, your specific dose of tirzepatide will be based on your health status, starting weight, and goals. While you’ll start with a low dose of tirzepatide at first, over time, it slowly increases so that you can reach your ideal weight safely and effectively. Around weeks four to six, the initial weight loss benefits of tirzepatide can be seen, especially when combined with a healthy diet and exercise. Some people on tirzepatide can lose up to 15% of their body weight.

## How Tirzepatide Helps with Appetite Suppression

If you want to know does tirzepatide suppress appetite, it does. How tirzepatide aids in appetite suppression is unique since a few different action methods are involved.

For starters, tirzepatide mimics the functionality of the hormone GLP-1, which regulates hunger, and the GIP hormone (glucose-dependent insulinotropic polypeptide) so that you eat less food. By consuming less food and calories, it is easier to lose weight. But since tirzepatide is designed to function like how these hormones work, it also leads to a feeling of fullness and less desire to eat.

Since tirzepatide also works to slow down the rate at which food is emptied into the intestines, food remains in the stomach for a more extended period, giving way to a feeling of satiation and more of a suppressed appetite.

In a clinical trial, overweight and obese adults taking tirzepatide reported less hunger and a decreased appetite. Those taking tirzepatide weekly noted more of a suppressed appetite and reduced food cravings compared to subjects taking liraglutide.

## Potential Side Effects and Considerations

Like all elective medications, there are potential risks and side effects to be aware of if you take tirzepatide, which may include:

- Nausea
- Diarrhea
- Decreased appetite
- Vomiting
- Constipation
- Indigestion
- Stomach pain
- Injection site reactions
- Tiredness
- Allergic reactions
- Hair loss
- Heartburn

More serious risks can occur with tirzepatide, which can cause thyroid issues, more severe stomach problems, kidney and gallbladder problems, pancreatitis, low blood sugar, vision changes, and depression.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How does tirzepatide suppress appetite?

Tirzepatide suppresses the appetite by mimicking the functionality of the GLP-1 and GIP hormones. After eating, the body naturally releases these hormones, sending a signal to the brain that the stomach is full. The appetite is suppressed since there’s less of a hungry feeling and more of a full one.

### How effective is tirzepatide in clinical trials for appetite suppression?

In clinical trials, tirzepatide has been shown to effectively suppress the appetite, leading to a feeling of fullness and fewer food cravings, for less eating and weight loss.

### Can tirzepatide be combined with other weight loss treatments?

The medication works well on its own and does not and should not need to be combined with other medical weight loss treatments.
